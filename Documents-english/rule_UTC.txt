Rules
📌 1. Match format
Each match consists of three rounds (each round lasts 3 minutes) between two teams.
The winning team is the one with the higher total score after all rounds.
Extra rounds may be held in case of a tie.
📌 2. Teams
Each team may have a maximum of 5 members and must include at least one student from the Mechanical Engineering or Mechatronics department of the university.
📌 3. Competition arena
The arena is circular with a diameter of 100 cm and a 5 cm white border.
There are two starting lines placed 20 cm apart.
📌 4. Robot regulations
Dimensions: maximum 15 cm × 15 cm, height is not limited.
Weight: ≤ 1.5 kg.
The robot must be fully autonomous; wireless communication during matches is prohibited.
The robot must include a 3-second delay in its program before it starts moving.
Robots must not cause interference, damage the arena, or use liquids, adhesives, chemicals, or any throwing/shooting mechanisms.
📌 5. Winning conditions
A robot wins the round by pushing the opponent out of the arena or causing it to flip over.
If both robots fall out of the arena, the robot that falls later wins.
If there is no winner, an extra round will be held; if still tied, the result will be decided by drawing lots.
📌 6. Violations
Robot regulation violations: match loss and the opponent receives +2 points.
Unsportsmanlike behavior: disqualification from the competition.
Match violations: warnings, or loss of the round or match depending on the number of violations.
📌 7. Protests and referees
The team captain may file a protest before or after a round.
The referee’s decision is final.

Notes
In regular matches, the starting positions of your robot and the opponent’s robot will be decided by the referee:
Front vs front
Side vs side
Rear vs rear
45-degree angle vs −45-degree angle

In the semifinals and finals, teams may choose their robot’s starting positions.
The arena is non-metallic, so magnets cannot be used in the robot.
