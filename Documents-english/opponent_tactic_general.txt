I. Opponent Overview
Most opponents belong to the Tanker category (high pushing force, slow movement), although there are still some faster robots (Speeder type).

Common characteristics:

Usually only the front is reinforced (dozer blade, wedge)
Center of mass shifted forward to optimize pushing force
Other areas such as:
Left side
Right side
Two rear corners
Rear side
are usually not well protected

This creates vulnerabilities when attacked from:

Both sides
The rear
Diagonal angles

II. Mechanical characteristics and collisions

If the opponent manages to get their wedge underneath:
Counter-attack capability is greatly reduced
Effective pushing force is lost
Tanker pushing force is usually:
Very strong
Focused on pushing power
Does not cause much damage (not destruction-type robots)
Some robots are not fully painted black:
This may affect IR sensor detection reliability
Could create an advantage if exploited properly

III. Electrical system and sensors

Common characteristics:

Use high-voltage batteries (typically 6S)
Very sensitive distance sensors
Fast detection sensors but usually simple processing logic

IV. Common strategies

Frequently observed patterns:

1. Random Search Pattern:
The robot moves randomly to find the opponent.

2. Spin Search Pattern:
The robot spins in place.
If the opponent is detected:
→ Charge straight forward

3. Slow Scan Pattern:
The robot moves slowly:
→ Detect
→ Align
→ Attack

Remarks:
Most strategies follow this pattern:
Detect → Align → Rush

Rarely seen:

Predictive movement
Adaptive strategy
Multi-state combat logic

Interesting observations:

Some robots with advanced strategies lose because their hardware is too simple or not well matched to the strategy

Many tanker-type robots
